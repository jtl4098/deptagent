
# Accommodation Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
TribalScale Inc. "The Company" is dedicated to removing and preventing barriers in the workplace for all employees, visitors and clients, and is committed to creating an environment that is accessible to all. Accommodation can include changes to hours of work, specialized equipment, modified duties, or other needs that arise related to a person's religion, gender, disability, family status or other human rights protected ground.

TribalScale strives to create and foster a work environment that is free of barriers based on age, race, ancestry, place of origin, colour, ethnic origin, citizenship, creed, sex (including pregnancy) gender identity, gender expression, sexual orientation, record of offences, marital status, family status and disability. TribalScale commits to providing accommodation for needs related to the grounds of the Ontario Human Rights Code and Workplace Safety Act, unless to do so would cause undue hardship.

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that all employees of TribalScale are aware of their rights and responsibilities under the Ontario Human Rights Code with respect to accommodation. This policy will also set out in writing TribalScale's procedures for requesting accommodation and the responsibilities of Managers, HR and Employees with respect to accessibility and accommodation. This policy is inclusive to all employees under, supported, and compensated by TribalScale Inc.

### 1.2 Scope
The accommodation policy applies to all TribalScale employees who may require accommodations, and to prospective employees who may require accommodation during the recruitment and selection process. In accordance with TribalScale's obligations under the AODA, the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act, 2005, Ontario Human Rights Code, 1990, and the Workplace Safety Act, 1995, the company will seek to reasonably accommodate employees by removing barriers that limit, restrict or prevent individuals with disabilities from participating in the workplace.

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All TribalScale employees and contractors
- Prospective employees during recruitment and selection
- Managers and supervisors
- Human Resources personnel
- Visitors and clients with accessibility needs

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Commitment to Accessibility
TribalScale is committed to creating an environment that is accessible to all by removing and preventing barriers in the workplace. The company will provide accommodation for needs related to protected grounds unless it would cause undue hardship.

### 3.2 Recruitment and Selection Accommodation
In accordance with the AODA, TribalScale will seek to reasonably eliminate barriers to employment during the recruitment and selection process:
- **Recruitment**: Notify job applicants that TribalScale has an accommodation policy and will provide accommodation to job applicants with disabilities during the hiring process
- **Selection**: When a job applicant is invited to participate in the selection process or to attend an interview, notify the candidate that accommodation for job applicants with disabilities during the assessment process is available upon request
- **Hiring**: In all offers of employment, employers must include a notice about its policies for accommodating employees with disabilities

### 3.3 Accessibility Statement
At TribalScale our company is built on doing things differently and challenging the status quo. We don't just support differences, we celebrate and thrive on them for the benefit of our employees, our solutions, and our partners. TribalScale is proud to be an equal opportunity employer and workplace. We are committed to equal employment opportunity regardless of race, colour, ancestry, religion, sex, national origin, sexual orientation, age, citizenship, marital status, able-bodiedness or gender identity. If you require accommodation for any reason, please let us know as we are more than happy to accommodate all needs you may have.

### 3.4 Assistive Devices and Support
TribalScale will ensure employees are trained and familiar with various assistive devices that may be used by employees or customers with disabilities while accessing our goods or services.

- **Communication**: TribalScale will communicate with people with disabilities in ways that take into account their disability
- **Service Animals**: TribalScale welcomes people with disabilities and their service animals. Service animals are allowed on the parts of our premises that are open to the public
- **Support Persons**: Individuals with a disability who are accompanied by a support person will be allowed to have that person accompany them on TribalScale premises

### 3.5 Return-to-Work Support
Employees who are returning to work after a prolonged time out of the office will be supported in their transition back to the workplace. Whether an employee is coming back to their original position, or returning to work with modified responsibilities, the below steps must be taken:
1. Establish a date of return
2. Discuss any accommodations (if needed)
3. Create a plan, in writing, for an employee's return to work

Return-to-work plans will be created on an individual basis in partnership with your manager and Human Resources.

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Employees
- Request accommodations through the Employee Accommodation Request Form
- Provide necessary documentation when requested
- Participate in the creation and revision of accommodation plans
- Maintain confidentiality of their accommodation needs
- Cooperate with the accommodation process

### 4.2 Managers and Supervisors
- Accept and process accommodation requests promptly
- Work with HR to develop accommodation plans
- Implement approved accommodations
- Maintain confidentiality of accommodation information
- Ensure workplace accessibility for their teams

### 4.3 Human Resources Department
- Process accommodation requests and documentation
- Develop individualized accommodation plans
- Ensure compliance with AODA, Human Rights Code, and Workplace Safety Act
- Provide guidance and support to managers and employees
- Maintain confidential records of accommodations

### 4.4 Executive Leadership
- Demonstrate commitment to accessibility and accommodation
- Provide resources and training for accommodation processes
- Review accommodation practices annually
- Ensure organizational compliance with accessibility legislation

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 Requesting Accommodation
1. Employee submits accommodation request through the Employee Accommodation Request Form to their manager
2. Manager forwards request to Human Resources department
3. HR consults with employee on how to best meet their individual needs
4. Accommodation plan is developed in partnership with employee, manager, and HR
5. Plan is implemented and monitored for effectiveness

### 5.2 Accommodation Plan Development
1. HR reviews the accommodation request and supporting documentation
2. Employee and manager participate in developing the accommodation plan
3. Plan includes specific accommodations, timeline, and responsibilities
4. Plan is documented and signed by all parties
5. Plan is reviewed regularly and updated as needed

### 5.3 Documentation Requirements
Documentation related to accommodation requests will be needed and should be discussed with your Manager or Human Resources when you meet. Any documentation of a personal or medical nature can be submitted to the Human Resources department, who will share (as appropriate) only information related to any work related restrictions or circumstances that require accommodation. The confidentiality of your personal and/or medical information will be safeguarded by the Human Resources department.

### 5.4 Return-to-Work Process
1. Employee notifies manager and HR of planned return date
2. Medical clearance and any restrictions are reviewed
3. Accommodation needs for return are assessed
4. Return-to-work plan is developed and documented
5. Transition support is provided as needed

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- Regular review of accommodation requests and outcomes
- Annual audit of accessibility compliance
- Training records for staff on accommodation procedures
- Documentation of undue hardship assessments
- Reporting on accommodation effectiveness

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- **Policy Violations**: Verbal warning and corrective action plan
- **Discrimination**: Formal investigation and disciplinary action
- **Failure to Accommodate**: Legal action and potential penalties
- **Breach of Confidentiality**: Disciplinary action up to termination
- **Legal Non-Compliance**: Regulatory fines and legal consequences

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions may be made only when accommodation would cause undue hardship:
- Significant difficulty or expense to the organization
- Substantial interference with business operations
- Health and safety risks to others
- Documented business justification
- Legal consultation required for exceptions
