
# Time Off Policy - United States

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This time off policy provides you flexibility when scheduling your time off. Employee time off, consists of vacation, holidays, birthday and sick time.

In addition to vacation, your time off includes ten (10) paid core holidays, seven(7) paid sick days (anytime taken after is unpaid), one (1) paid birthday and the paid Holiday shut down during Christmas and New Year, as illustrated below.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All regular full-time and part-time employees in the United States
- All locations and remote work arrangements
- All types of paid and unpaid leave including Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
- Both exempt and non-exempt employees
- Federal, state, and local legal requirements

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All US-based employees
- Managers and supervisors
- HR personnel
- Payroll administrators
- Legal and compliance teams

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Eligibility Requirements
Employees must work at least 30 hours per week to be eligible for vacation time off work. If you are a regular employee working at least 30 hours per week, you are eligible for vacation, holidays, and sick time and will begin to accrue vacation on your date of hire. If you work under 30 hours per week, you will receive vacation pay at a rate of 6% of time worked. You begin to accrue vacation time on your date of hire. If you have any questions please reach out to hr@tribalscale.com.

### 3.2 Statutory Paid Company Holidays
Take time with family and friends to celebrate TribalScale's core holidays. When a legal public holiday falls on a Saturday, it is usually observed on the preceding Friday for pay and leave purposes. When the holiday falls on a Sunday, it is usually observed on the following Monday for pay and leave purposes.

| Independence Day | July 3rd |
| Labor Day | September 7th |

### 3.3 Holiday Shut Down
TribalScale provides paid Holiday shut down during Christmas and New Year, typically 3-5 days depending on the year and business requirements.

### 3.4 Annual Vacation Carryover
On January 1st of a New Year, for any earned but unused vacation time, in your vacation bank, you will automatically carry over up to 5 days vacation time, from one calendar year to the next. Any unused vacation time in excess of 5 days, cannot be carried over and is forfeited, with the exceptions noted below.

### 3.5 Vacation Upon Termination
Subject to the terms of this policy, TribalScale will pay for any accrued unused vacation days as of your date of termination. Any other time off is not eligible for payment.

### 3.6 Time Off Requests
Prior approval by your manager for taking any time off (e.g., vacation, sick day, birthday, etc) is required and must be recorded in BambooHR. If you need 1 week off, you need to send a request at least (1) month in advance. If you need 2 weeks off, you need to send a request at least (2) months in advance and so on.

### 3.7 Birthday Time Off
Employees are eligible for one (1) day off per calendar year on their birthday. Please request your birthday off at least (2) weeks in advance through BambooHR. Birthday time off is paid at your regular base rate of pay. This time is not to be used as vacation time. If your birthday falls on a weekend, speak to your manager to get another day off. It will be up to the manager's discretion to approve a different day off, instead your actual birthday.

### 3.8 Sick Time
Employees are eligible for unlimited sick days, up to 7 days employer paid per calendar year. After the 7 days, any additional time off requested as sick time will be unpaid. Time off includes illness, hospitalization, and doctor appointments. Sick time is paid at your regular base rate of pay. This time is not to be used as vacation time. Sick time cannot be payout or carried forward at the end of year.

### 3.9 Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
As a TribalScale employee in the US, you are eligible for up to 12 unpaid weeks per year, of job protected family/personal emergency/medical leave in accordance to the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993. Medical and/or Emergency Leave and must be approved by both your manager and HR prior to the leave. To qualify for emergency leave, you must notify HR as well as your manager the following points:
- The duration or expected duration of the absence
- Date you (as the employee) were seen by a healthcare professional
- Whether or not you (as the employee) were examined in person by a health care professional

### 3.10 Bereavement Leave
TribalScale provides paid time off in the event of the death of specified family members. An employee is entitled up to five (5) consecutive working days of paid leave for immediate family and up to three (3) consecutive working days of paid leave for non-immediate family. Bereavement leave must be entered in BambooHR under Bereavement.

**Immediate Family (up to 5 consecutive work days):**
- Spouse, Qualified Domestic Partner
- Parent, Parent-in-law, Step-parent
- Sibling
- Brother-/Sister-in-law
- Child, Stepchild, Foster Child
- Grandparent, Grandparent-in-law, Grandchildren
- Ex-spouse

**Non-Immediate Family (up to 3 consecutive work days):**
- Aunt, Uncle
- Niece, Nephew
- Foster parent

*If additional time off is required employees should work with their managers and HR.

Questions about this Policy should be raised with HR at hr@tribalsclae.com.

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Employees
- Request time off through established procedures
- Provide reasonable notice for scheduling
- Communicate coverage plans with team
- Return from leave as scheduled
- Maintain contact during extended absences

### 4.2 Managers and Supervisors
- Approve time off requests based on business needs
- Ensure adequate coverage during absences
- Monitor leave usage and patterns
- Support employees returning from leave
- Document leave-related performance issues

### 4.3 HR Department
- Administer leave programs and track accruals
- Ensure compliance with federal and state laws
- Process FMLA and ADA accommodations
- Provide guidance on leave options
- Maintain confidential leave records

### 4.4 Payroll Department
- Process time off payments accurately
- Track accruals and usage
- Ensure proper coding of leave types
- Coordinate with benefits during unpaid leave
- Provide reporting on leave utilization

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 Requesting Time Off
1. Employee submits request through HR system or manager
2. Include dates, reason, and coverage plan
3. Manager reviews for business impact
4. Approval or denial communicated within 48 hours
5. Time off entered into scheduling system

### 5.2 FMLA Process
1. Employee notifies HR of need for FMLA leave
2. HR provides FMLA paperwork and certification
3. Medical certification obtained within 15 days
4. Leave designation and tracking begins
5. Employee rights and obligations explained

### 5.3 Return from Leave
1. Employee contacts HR/manager before return date
2. Medical clearance obtained if required
3. Updated job description reviewed if needed
4. Transitional support provided
5. Performance monitoring during transition

### 5.4 Leave of Absence Administration
1. Request submitted with supporting documentation
2. HR reviews eligibility and requirements
3. Approval obtained from appropriate management
4. Benefits coordination arranged
5. Return-to-work planning initiated

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- Regular review of leave utilization patterns
- Compliance audits with federal and state laws
- Accrual calculations verified quarterly
- FMLA case management and tracking
- Annual policy effectiveness assessment

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- **Administrative Errors**: Correction and training
- **Policy Violations**: Verbal warning and retraining
- **Abuse of Leave**: Written warning and leave restrictions
- **FMLA Interference**: Legal action and penalties
- **Repeated Violations**: Progressive disciplinary action

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions require:
- Business justification documentation
- Senior management approval
- Legal consultation when applicable
- Additional controls or monitoring
- Regular reassessment of exception
