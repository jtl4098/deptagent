
# Anti-Violence and Harassment Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
TribalScale ("TribalScale") or "the Company") is committed to provide a safe and respectful work environment that promotes the dignity and self-esteem of each individual. The Company will not tolerate Workplace Violence and/or Harassment, based on a prohibited ground, such as race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, marital status, family status, disability, pardoned conviction, or any other classification protected by law.

Management will take all complaints of Workplace Violence and/or Harassment seriously. This policy is not designed or intended to limit the Company's authority to discipline or take remedial action for workplace conduct that TribalScale deems unacceptable, regardless of whether that conduct satisfies the legal definition of discrimination or harassment.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to TribalScale employees, consultants, contractors, interns, and co-op students. This also applies to the conduct of all workers towards customers and suppliers. For the purpose of this policy "Workplace" shall be defined broadly to include all places, locations or premises where workers are required, directed or permitted to conduct business or perform services on behalf of the Company and includes all work-related settings and activities, whether inside or outside the workplace, and includes business trips and business-related social.

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All employees and contractors
- Managers and supervisors
- HR personnel
- Third-party service providers
- Visitors and clients

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Workplace Violence | The exercise of physical force by a person against a worker, in a workplace, that causes or could cause physical injury to the worker. It also includes an attempt to exercise physical force against a worker in a workplace that could cause physical injury, or a statement or behavior that a worker could reasonably interpret as a threat to exercise physical force |
| Workplace Harassment | A form of prohibited discrimination. It may be defined as any course of vexatious comment or conduct that is known or ought reasonably to be known to be unwelcome. Workplace Harassment includes discrimination or harassment as defined in the Human Rights Code or based on prohibited grounds contained in the Code. Workplace Harassment also includes sexual harassment or solicitation |
| Prohibited Grounds | Race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, marital status, family status, disability, pardoned conviction, or any other classification protected by law |
| Human Rights Code | Provincial legislation protecting against discrimination and harassment |
| Bill 168 | Ontario legislation requiring employers to address workplace violence and harassment |
| OSHA | Occupational Safety and Health Administration guidelines for workplace safety |
| Workplace Safety Act | Provincial legislation ensuring workplace health and safety |

### 2.1 Workplace Violence Examples
Workplace violence includes but is not limited to:
- Verbally threatening to attack a worker
- Leaving threatening notes at or sending threatening emails to a workplace
- Shaking a fist in a worker's face
- Wielding a weapon at work
- Hitting or trying to hit a worker
- Sexual violence against a worker
- Kicking an object the worker is standing on such as a ladder
- Trying to run down a worker using a vehicle or equipment

### 2.2 Workplace Harassment Examples
Workplace harassment includes but is not limited to:
- Remarks, epithets, slurs, negative stereotyping, jokes, innuendoes, written or graphic material, or taunting
- Threatening, intimidating or hostile acts
- Written or graphic material that denigrates or shows hostility toward an individual or group
- Displays of offensive pictures or cartoons
- Derogatory or other offensive material at work, by any medium including by downloading from the internet
- Persisting in asking for a date after having been refused
- Written or oral references to sexual conduct or gossip regarding one's sex life
- Offensive comments
- Unwelcome sexual advances whether they involve physical touching or not
- Making remarks, jokes or innuendos that demean, ridicule, intimidate, or offend
- Displaying or circulating offensive pictures or materials in print or electronic form
- Bullying
- Repeated offensive or intimidating phone calls or e-mails

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Zero Tolerance Policy
TribalScale is committed to provide a safe and respectful work environment that promotes the dignity and self-esteem of each individual. The Company will not tolerate Workplace Violence and/or Harassment, based on a prohibited ground, such as race, national or ethnic origin, colour, religion, age, sex, sexual orientation, marital status, family status, disability, pardoned conviction, or any other classification protected by law.

Management will take all complaints of Workplace Violence and/or Harassment seriously in order to ensure employees feel safe and secure in the workplace.

### 3.2 Reporting Requirements
Complaints of unlawful discrimination, harassment, and/or violence should be brought to the employee's manager, Human Resources department, or a leadership member you feel most comfortable with. All managers and leadership members receiving such complaints must bring the complaint forward to Human Resources.

Although reports may be made verbally, employees are strongly encouraged to make such reports in writing. Written reports will assist the investigation process. The written complaint should set out:
- Your name, position and contact information
- The nature of the complaint - harassment, discrimination, etc.
- Name and position of the alleged discriminator/harasser
- The incident(s) and actions that are the basis of the complaint, including dates, locations, persons involved and acts or communications that are the subject of the complaint
- The resolution being sought
- Names of any witnesses and any documents that are relevant or that corroborate

No record of the complaint will be placed in your personnel file, as long as it was made in good faith.

### 3.3 Investigation Process
When a complaint is received, Human Resources will promptly investigate the allegation as discreetly and confidentially as possible. Where appropriate, at TribalScale's sole discretion, TribalScale may appoint an outside investigator to assist in the investigation. TribalScale will have the sole discretion to choose the investigator and to decide when such a referral is appropriate.

The investigation will normally include a private interview with the complainant and with witnesses, as appropriate. An investigation may proceed even where the employee/complainant is either reluctant to proceed with a formal complaint or withdraws a formal complaint.

Employees are expected to cooperate in the investigation of a complaint and efforts to resolve it.

When the investigation is complete, Human Resources to the extent appropriate, will inform the person filing the complaint and the person alleged to have committed the conduct of the results of the investigation.

### 3.4 Disciplinary Actions
If the Company determines that a violation of the policy has occurred, it will take disciplinary action as it deems appropriate, including but not limited to counseling, warnings, transfers, suspensions and termination of employment, including termination, without notice, for just cause.

If the Company determines that the conduct warrants discipline even though it does not meet the definitions in this policy, the Company can and will take the disciplinary action it deems appropriate.

### 3.5 Resolution Timeline
With necessary modification, the procedures for the reporting and investigation of Workplace Harassment and/or Violence incidents or complaints will apply to all cases of alleged Harassment and/or Violence regardless of whether the complaint is made against another employee, member of management, or a third party (e.g., customers, vendors, partners or contractors).

When a complaint is made, the Company will make all reasonable efforts to conclude the investigation and resolve the issue as soon as possible. Employees are encouraged to report incidents of Workplace Harassment promptly.

### 3.6 Reprisal Protection
All employees are protected from reprisal or threats of reprisal for exercising their rights under this policy in good faith. Anyone who retaliates in any way against a person who has exercised their rights will be subject to discipline.

### 3.7 Malicious Complaints
Should the complaint be made in bad faith, be malicious and without basis in fact, this can lead to disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment, including termination, without notice, for just cause.

### 3.8 Confidentiality
TribalScale will maintain the confidentiality of reports, investigations and the results of the investigation to the extent practicable. Employees involved in reporting and/or investigations should also understand the importance of maintaining confidentiality.

Information obtained about an incident or complaint of a workplace harassment, including identifying information about any individuals involved, will not be disclosed unless the disclosure is necessary for the purposes of investigating or taking corrective action with respect to the incident or complaint, or otherwise required by law.

### 3.9 Right of Access to Outside Tribunals
It is an employee's right to file a complaint directly with the provincial Human Rights Commission or under the Occupational Health and Safety Act, as applicable.

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 All Employees
It is the responsibility of everyone at TribalScale to act in compliance with this Policy and to assist in ensuring that we have, and continue to have a workplace free from harassment. In particular, it is the responsibility of all workers to work in compliance with this policy and any supporting programs, policies or procedures. Workers are encouraged to report any incident of Workplace Harassment as outlined below.

### 4.2 Managers and Supervisors
It is the responsibility of Managers and Supervisors to adhere to this policy and any supporting policies or programs and to help ensure that all Company measures and procedures are followed by workers.

### 4.3 HR Department
- Administer the anti-violence and harassment program
- Conduct investigations and provide guidance
- Maintain confidential records of complaints and investigations
- Coordinate with external investigators when needed
- Ensure compliance with Bill 168, OSHA, and Workplace Safety Act

### 4.4 Executive Leadership
- Demonstrate commitment to violence and harassment-free workplace
- Provide resources for program implementation
- Support disciplinary actions when violations occur
- Communicate organizational values regarding respect and safety

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 If You Believe You Have Been Subject to Workplace Harassment or are a Witness to Workplace Harassment
- If you feel you can speak to the person(s) involved, try to do so. Tell the person the behavior is inappropriate and ask them to stop.
- If the inappropriate behavior continues or you do not feel you can speak directly to the person, you can report the matter pursuant to Procedure as set out below.

### 5.2 Making a Complaint
- Complaints of unlawful discrimination, harassment, and/or violence should be brought to the employee's manager, Human Resources department, or a leadership member you feel most comfortable with. All managers and leadership members receiving such complaints must bring the complaint forward to Human Resources. Although reports may be made verbally, employees are strongly encouraged to make such reports in writing. Written reports will assist the investigation process.

### 5.3 Written Complaint Requirements
The written complaint should set out:
- Your name, position and contact information
- The nature of the complaint - harassment, discrimination, etc.
- Name and position of the alleged discriminator/harasser
- The incident(s) and actions that are the basis of the complaint, including dates, locations, persons involved and acts or communications that are the subject of the complaint
- The resolution being sought
- Names of any witnesses and any documents that are relevant or that corroborate

### 5.4 If You Are Accused of Harassment
- If you did not intend to offend, apologize.
- You will be given full opportunity to respond at an appropriate stage of the resolution.

### 5.5 The Resolution Process
- When a complaint is received, Human Resources will promptly investigate the allegation as discreetly and confidentially as possible. Where appropriate, at TribalScale's sole discretion, TribalScale may appoint an outside investigator to assist in the investigation. TribalScale will have the sole discretion to choose the investigator and to decide when such a referral is appropriate.
- The investigation will normally include a private interview with the complainant and with witnesses, as appropriate. An investigation may proceed even where the employee/complainant is either reluctant to proceed with a formal complaint or withdraws a formal complaint.
- Employees are expected to cooperate in the investigation of a complaint and efforts to resolve it.
- When the investigation is complete, Human Resources to the extent appropriate, will inform the person filing the complaint and the person alleged to have committed the conduct of the results of the investigation.

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- Regular review of harassment reports and trends
- Annual program effectiveness assessment
- Training completion tracking
- Compliance with legal requirements
- Continuous improvement initiatives

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- **Policy Violations**: Verbal warning and training
- **Minor Harassment**: Written warning and disciplinary action
- **Serious Harassment**: Suspension or termination
- **Retaliation**: Immediate disciplinary action up to termination
- **False Reports**: Counseling and potential disciplinary action

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions to standard procedures require:
- Legal consultation
- Senior leadership approval
- Documentation of business justification
- Additional safeguards implemented
- Regular monitoring of exception
