
# Workplace Violence Prevention Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This Policy is an extension of the TribalScale's Anti Harassment policy and is for employees located in all offices. TribalScale ("TribalScale" or "the Company") is committed to providing a safe and healthy Workplace that is free from actual, attempted, or threatened Violence.

The safety and security of TribalScale's personnel, customers, vendors, and visitors are of vital importance to the Company. To that end, TribalScale will not tolerate any form of Workplace Violence.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to all TribalScale employees, consultants, contractors, interns, and co-op students (collectively referred to as "employees" for purposes of this policy). This also applies to the conduct of employees towards customers and suppliers. For the purpose of this policy, "Workplace" shall be defined broadly to include all places, locations or premises where employees are required, directed or permitted to conduct business or perform services on behalf of the Company and includes all work-related settings and activities, whether inside or outside the workplace, and includes business trips and business-related social events.

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All employees and contractors
- Managers and supervisors
- Security and facilities personnel
- HR team members
- Emergency response personnel

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|

An attempt to exercise physical force against a staff member in the Workplace. 3. A statement or behaviour that it is reasonable for an employee to interpret as a Workplace Violence. 4. ## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Zero Tolerance Policy
The Company has a zero tolerance policy for Workplace Violence. Those found in violation of this policy will be subject to appropriate discipline up to and including termination from employment.

### 3.2 No Weapons Policy
No weapons of any type are allowed on Company premises including, but not limited to, parking areas owned or used by the Company. This prohibition against weapons applies whether the weapon is on the individual, carried in a container or openly, or is in a vehicle brought onto the Company premises. Furthermore, while on Company business, employees are prohibited from carrying or otherwise possessing or transporting weapons at any time and at any place (including Company vehicles).

### 3.3 Consequences of Policy Violation
Any person who engages in Workplace Violence or carries a weapon, or otherwise violates this policy, shall be removed from the premises as quickly as safety permits and shall be barred from the Company premises until the outcome of an investigation. Violations of this policy shall result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. In addition, in appropriate cases, the Company may contact law enforcement authorities or other third parties for assistance, arrest or prosecution. No other Company practice, policy or procedure should be interpreted to limit the Company's ability to take prompt and appropriate action to prevent Workplace Violence or otherwise enforce this policy.

### 3.4 Customer Background Check Requests
TribalScale's customers sometimes request that TribalScale certify that all TribalScale employees working onsite at the customer's facilities (as well as those who access customer data or information), have successfully passed a background screening, including criminal background check, either on an annual basis or prior to their commencement of work for the customer. If such additional screening is deemed necessary, your supervisor will notify you in accordance.

### 3.5 Searches and Surveillance
Searches of employee offices, desks, lockers, briefcases and bags, equipment, emails and other items may be necessary in connection with investigations under this policy. In addition, the Company may implement any security measures deemed necessary, including video surveillance, in order to enforce this policy. Such actions will be taken by the Company in accordance with applicable federal, provincial, and local laws.

### 3.6 Investigation Procedures
All incidents of Workplace Violence will be promptly investigated, as confidentially as possible based upon the circumstances. TribalScale will only release information as necessary to investigate and respond to the complaint or as required by law. Employees questioned as part of an investigation will also be asked and expected to treat all discussions as confidential. Complete anonymity or confidentiality cannot be guaranteed. An investigation will commence as quickly as possible. All incidents will be investigated by an investigator(s) chosen by TribalScale in its sole discretion. TribalScale may choose, in its sole discretion, to use either an internal or external investigator, or to involve the police depending on the nature of the complaint.

The investigation may include as appropriate:
a) a documented interview with the complainant/victim;
b) a documented interview with the alleged perpetrator, which will include an opportunity to respond to the allegations.
c) documented interviews with any witnesses
d) the reviewing of any related documentation, policies or procedures; and
e) any other step(s) deemed necessary by the investigator(s) to fully and fairly investigate the incident.

At the conclusion of the investigation, the investigator will make suggestions or recommendations, about how to respond to the specific incident as well as how to better prevent similar incidents. In an appropriate case, the investigator may prepare a written report of the findings of fact and conclusions. All documents related to an investigation, including the initial complaint (if in written form), notes from meetings and interviews, lists of conclusions and recommendations and any written report from the investigator will be treated, as far as possible, as confidential and private and will be placed in a separate file. Complete confidentiality or privacy cannot be guaranteed. The reporting and investigation procedures laid out in this Policy shall apply (with necessary modifications) to all cases of alleged or reported incidents of Workplace Violence, regardless of whether the complaint is made by or against an employee, member of management, or a third party.

A reported incident of Workplace Violence, may be investigated even if withdrawn by the employee/victim or even if the employee/victim chooses not to make a formal complaint. TribalScale will determine in its sole discretion whether or not to proceed with an investigation in such circumstances. In appropriate cases, the Company's response to any report of Workplace Violence may include the involvement of the police, other law enforcement officials, medical professionals or other experts as reasonably required.

### 3.7 Restraining or Other Orders
An employee who is protected or covered by a restraining order or other type of protective order must immediately provide a copy of the order to his or her manager or Human Resources Representative. The Company will strive to keep the order and its terms as confidential as is reasonably practicable under the circumstances.

### 3.8 Retaliation Prohibited
No retaliation will be tolerated against any person for reporting in good faith a violation of this policy or for filing, testifying, assisting or participating in any investigation, proceeding or hearing conducted by the Company, or by law enforcement agency, tribunal or court.

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 All Employees
It is the responsibility of everyone at the Workplace to act in compliance with this Policy and to assist in ensuring that we have and continue to have a Workplace free from Violence. In particular, it is the responsibility of all workers to work in compliance with this policy.

### 4.2 Company Responsibilities
It is the responsibility of the Company to ensure that this policy and the supporting program are implemented and maintained and that all staff have appropriate information and instruction to protect themselves.

### 4.3 Managers and Supervisors
It is the responsibility of Managers and Supervisors to adhere to this policy and any supporting policies or programs and to ensure that all Company measures and procedures are followed by workers.

### 4.4 Reporting Responsibilities
All employees are responsible for immediately notifying their immediate supervisor, or any member of management, including Human Resources, of any violation or potential violation of this policy, including any threats that they have witnessed, received, or otherwise known about. Even without an expressed verbal threat, employees should alert management to any suspicious behavior that they suspect might pose a danger to employees or any other individuals, or to Company property.

● In situations where violence is in progress, has occurred, or is imminent, dial 911 to call the local law enforcement. You should also call your manager and local Human Resources Representative as soon as reasonably possible.
● For all potentially threatening situations where violence is not in progress or imminent, call your Human Resources Representative.
● If you are unable to contact your Human Resources Representative, contact your manager or any other member of management.
● The Company will strive to handle reports made under the policy with as much confidentiality as is reasonably practicable.

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 Incident Reporting
1. Employee or witness identifies violent incident or threat
2. Report made immediately through emergency channels
3. Security/HR assesses immediate threat level
4. Law enforcement notified if criminal activity suspected
5. Incident documented and investigation initiated

### 5.2 Threat Assessment
1. Reported threat evaluated for credibility and immediacy
2. Risk assessment conducted by trained personnel
3. Appropriate response measures implemented
4. Communication plan activated
5. Follow-up monitoring established

### 5.3 Emergency Response
1. Active threat identified or reported
2. Emergency response procedures activated
3. Employees directed to safe locations
4. Law enforcement response coordinated
5. All-clear procedures followed

### 5.4 Investigation Process
1. Incident details documented thoroughly
2. Interviews conducted with involved parties
3. Evidence collected and preserved
4. Findings analyzed and recommendations made
5. Corrective actions implemented

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- Regular review of violence prevention measures
- Training completion and effectiveness tracking
- Incident trend analysis and reporting
- Annual program assessment
- Compliance with legal requirements

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- **Minor Violations**: Verbal warning and additional training
- **Threats/Intimidation**: Immediate suspension and investigation
- **Physical Violence**: Termination and criminal prosecution
- **Failure to Report**: Disciplinary action for negligence
- **Retaliation**: Immediate termination

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions require:
- Senior leadership approval
- Legal consultation
- Enhanced security measures
- Documentation of business justification
- Regular reassessment
