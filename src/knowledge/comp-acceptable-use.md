
# Acceptable Use Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
To remain competitive, better serve our customers and provide office users with the best tools to do their jobs, TribalScale Inc. makes available to our workforce access to one or more forms of electronic media and services, including computers, e-mail, telephones, voicemail, fax machines, external electronic bulletin boards, wire services, online services, intranet, Internet and the World Wide Web.

TribalScale Inc. encourages the use of these media and associated services because they can make communication more efficient and effective and because they are valuable sources of information about vendors, customers, technology, and new products and services. However, all office users and everyone connected with the organization should remember that electronic media and services provided by the company are company property and their purpose is to facilitate and support company business. All computer users have the responsibility to use these resources in a professional, ethical, and lawful manner.

To ensure that all office users are responsible, the following guidelines have been established for using e-mail the Internet and any other form of electronic communication (the "Electronic Resources"). No policy can lay down rules to cover every possible situation. Instead, it is designed to express TribalScale Inc. philosophy and set forth general principles when using electronic media and services.

The Electronic Resources provided by TribalScale Inc. are for business use to assist office users in the performance of their jobs. Office users are expected to demonstrate a sense of responsibility and not abuse this privilege.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All TribalScale employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary workers (referred to as "office users")
- All electronic media and services provided by TribalScale Inc., including computers, e-mail, telephones, voicemail, fax machines, external electronic bulletin boards, wire services, online services, intranet, Internet and the World Wide Web
- All computing devices and networks owned or managed by TribalScale
- All access to TribalScale systems, whether on company premises or remotely
- Personal devices used for TribalScale business (BYOD - Bring Your Own Device)

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All employees and contractors
- IT and security personnel
- Management and supervisors
- Third-party service providers with system access

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Prohibited Communications
Electronic Resources cannot be used for knowingly transmitting, retrieving, or storing any communication that is:
- Discriminatory or harassing
- Derogatory to any individual or group
- Obscene, sexually explicit or pornographic
- Defamatory or threatening
- In violation of any license governing the use of software
- Used for any purpose that is illegal or contrary to TribalScale Inc. policy or business interests

### 3.2 Prohibited Uses
Electronic Resources cannot be used to:
1. Violate any law of any applicable jurisdiction, including, without limitation, laws governing advertising, alcohol, antitrust, child protection, drugs, encryption, exportation, food, financial services, firearms, gambling, importation, information systems, intellectual property, obscenity, privacy, securities, telecommunications and tobacco
2. Commit a tortuous or otherwise wrongful act, including, without limitation, the posting or communication of libelous, defamatory, scandalous, threatening, harassing, or private information without the permission of the person(s) involved, or posting content that is likely to cause emotional distress, whether through content, frequency or size
3. Engage in or facilitate gambling activities
4. Post, send, or receive any content that is obscene, pornographic, lewd, lascivious, or excessively violent
5. Offer, solicit, buy, sell, rent or license any goods, products, services, or information in, from, or to any location in which such activity is unlawful
6. Advocate, promote, or otherwise encourage violence against any government, organization, group, individual or property, or to provide instruction, information, or assistance in causing or carrying out such violence
7. Post, send, receive, display, distribute, or execute any content, including, without limitation, text, graphics, images, music, recordings, computer programs, links, frames, and "meta tags," that violates any copyright, right of publicity, patent, trademark, service mark, trade name, mask work, trade secret or other intellectual property right of others
8. Delete or alter author attributions, copyright notices, or trademark notices, unless expressly permitted in writing by the owner
9. Violate the terms of applicable software licensing agreements
10. Obtain or attempt to obtain unauthorized access, such as attempting to circumvent or circumventing any authentication or other security feature of any system, network or account. This includes accessing data not intended for the user, logging into a server or account the user is not authorized to access, or probing the security of any system, network, or account
11. Interfere or attempt to interfere with service to any user, host, or network by use of any program, script, command, or otherwise. This includes "denial of service" attacks, "flooding" of networks, deliberate attempts to overload a service or to burden excessively a service's resources, and attempts to "crash" a host
12. Introduce viruses, worms, harmful code and/or Trojan horses
13. Attempt to circumvent the approval process for posting to a moderated newsgroup or bulletin board or to attempt to evade spam filters
14. Cancel or supersede posts other than your own, with the exception of official newsgroup or bulletin board moderators performing their duties
15. Send or post unsolicited messages or e-mail, whether commercial or not, a) to any recipients who have requested that messages not be sent to them, or b) to a large number or recipients, including users, newsgroups, or bulletin boards, at one time
16. Send or post a message whose subject or content is unrelated to the subject matter of the newsgroup or bulletin board to which it is posted
17. Send or post a message or e-mail with deceptive, absent, or forged header or sender identification information
18. Propagate chain letters and pyramid schemes, whether or not the recipient wishes to receive such mailings
19. Use Internet Relay Chat "bots"
20. Hold TribalScale Inc., its affiliates, officers, office users and/or shareholders up to public scorn or ridicule
21. Resell TribalScale Inc.'s services, in whole or in part, to any entity or individual, without TribalScale Inc.'s prior written consent, or to misrepresent your relationship with TribalScale Inc.

### 3.3 Monitoring and Access
TribalScale Inc. reserves the right to monitor and collect information communicated, created, received or stored using its Electronic Resources, for the following purposes:
1. Cost analysis
2. Resource allocation
3. Optimum technical management of information resources
4. Detecting patterns of use that indicate office users are violating company policies or engaging in illegal activity
5. Monitor the use of the Electronic Resources to ensure they are being used in compliance with the law, this policy and other company policies
6. Office users should not assume electronic communications are completely private. Accordingly, if they have sensitive personal information to transmit, they should use other means.

### 3.4 Software and Downloads
To prevent computer viruses from being transmitted through the company's Electronic Resources, unauthorized downloading of any unauthorized software is strictly prohibited. Only software registered through TribalScale Inc. may be downloaded. Office users should contact the system administrator if they have any questions.

### 3.5 Security and Appropriate Use
Office users must respect the confidentiality of other individuals' electronic communications. Except in cases in which explicit authorization has been granted by company management, office users are prohibited from engaging in, or attempting to engage in:
1. Monitoring or intercepting the files or electronic communications of other office users or third parties
2. Hacking or obtaining access to systems or accounts they are not authorized to use
3. Using other people's log-ins or passwords
4. Breaching, testing, or monitoring computer or network security measures

No e-mail or other electronic communications can be sent that attempt to hide the identity of the sender or represent the sender as someone else.

Electronic media and services should not be used in a manner that is likely to cause network congestion or significantly hamper the ability of other people to access and use the system.

Anyone obtaining electronic access to other companies' or individuals' materials must respect all copyrights and cannot copy, retrieve, modify or forward copyrighted materials except as permitted by the copyright owner.

Personal use of Electronic Resources is permitted provided the use does not interfere with performance of the employee's duties or responsibilities or otherwise violate TribalScale policies or applicable law.

### 3.6 Encryption
Office users can use encryption software supplied to them by the systems administrator for purposes of safeguarding sensitive or confidential business information. Office users who use encryption on files stored on a company computer must provide their supervisor with a sealed hard copy record (to be retained in a secure location) of all of the passwords and/or encryption keys necessary to access the files.

### 3.7 Online Forums and Media Participation
Office users should remember that any messages or information sent on company-provided Electronic Resources to one or more individuals via an electronic network—for example, Internet mailing lists, bulletin boards, and online services—are statements identifiable and attributable to TribalScale Inc.

TribalScale Inc. recognizes that participation in some forums might be important to the performance of an office user's job. For instance, an office user might find the answer to a technical problem by consulting members of a news group devoted to the technical area.

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Employees and Users
- Comply with all policy requirements
- Report policy violations and security incidents
- Protect their login credentials and devices
- Use IT resources responsibly and ethically
- Participate in security awareness training

### 4.2 Managers and Supervisors
- Ensure their team members understand and follow the policy
- Address policy violations promptly
- Support security awareness initiatives
- Approve exceptions when appropriate
- Model appropriate use of IT resources

### 4.3 IT Department
- Provide technical controls and monitoring
- Maintain approved software and hardware lists
- Respond to security incidents and violations
- Provide user training and support
- Implement security tools and updates

### 4.4 Information Security Team
- Develop and maintain the Acceptable Use Policy
- Monitor compliance and investigate violations
- Provide guidance on security best practices
- Conduct regular security assessments
- Report on policy effectiveness

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 Account Setup and Management
1. New users complete security awareness training
2. IT creates accounts with appropriate access levels
3. Users receive login credentials and policy acknowledgment
4. Multi-factor authentication is configured
5. Regular password changes are enforced

### 5.2 Incident Reporting
1. User identifies potential policy violation or security incident
2. Incident reported to IT Help Desk or Security Team
3. Security Team assesses and investigates the incident
4. Corrective actions are taken as needed
5. Incident documented for future reference

### 5.3 Software Installation Requests
1. User submits request with business justification
2. IT reviews request for security and compatibility
3. If approved, software is installed or provided
4. User acknowledges responsibility for proper use
5. Installation logged for audit purposes

### 5.4 Remote Access Setup
1. User requests remote access with business justification
2. IT configures secure VPN access
3. Multi-factor authentication is required
4. User receives security guidelines for remote work
5. Access is monitored and logged

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- System access and usage are logged and monitored
- Regular audits of user activity and compliance
- Automated alerts for suspicious behavior
- Annual review of access rights and permissions
- Compliance reporting to management

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
Any office user who violates this policy will be subject to corrective action, including possible termination of employment, legal action, and criminal liability.

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions to this policy require:
- Written business justification
- Approval from department manager
- Review by IT and Security teams
- Documentation of compensating controls
- Regular review of exception validity
