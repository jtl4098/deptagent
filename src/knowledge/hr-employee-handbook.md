## Chapter 1: Complaint Resolution

### Harassment Complaints
All allegations of harassment will be immediately investigated and handled as confidentially as possible.

If it is determined that sexual harassment has occurred, immediate corrective action will be taken, which may result in termination.

All complaints will invariably culminate in a final interview with the aggrieved by Human Resources or with his/her immediate manager.

## Chapter 2: Work Week and Holidays

### Work Week
Our basic work week is composed of five working days – Monday to Friday. All full-time employees work a minimum of 37.5 regularly scheduled hours per week.

Our core business hours are from 9 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. each day. Working hours are flexible and with the employee's Manager's approval, you may start the day anytime between 8:00 a.m. and 9:00 a.m. at half-hour intervals.

### General Holidays
All Full-Time employees in the Canadian office are entitled to 10 (ten) paid holidays per year as follows:

- New Year's Day
- Family Day
- Good Friday
- Victoria Day
- Canada Day
- Civic Day
- Labour Day
- Thanksgiving Day
- Christmas Day
- Boxing Day

An approved holiday schedule is published by December 31 of each year. This schedule indicates the dates of holidays for the following calendar year.

### Overtime
Employees are expected to meet the job requirements during the normal hours of work. Overtime is therefore discouraged and is subject to management's discretion in exceptional circumstances only and it is subject to its written prior approval.

## Chapter 3: Leaves of Absence

### General
Any paid or unpaid leave of absence for valid personal reasons may be granted at the Corporation's discretion. The employee must apply in writing to his/her manager and Human Resources for a leave of absence, stating the reason for the leave, the start date and duration of the leave. Where possible, the employee should give at least four weeks of notice so that arrangements can be made to find a replacement.

Vacation entitlements mentioned in this section apply to the Full-Time employees only. Part Time or Hourly rated employees are governed in accordance with their employment contracts.

### Attendance and Absenteeism
TribalScale expects punctuality and good attendance by all employees. We recognize that sometimes there are legitimate reasons to be absent or late for work. However, poor attendance and/or chronic absenteeism places hardship on others to perform work normally assigned to the absent individual. It also compromises client service levels and creates risks for clients and internal stakeholders.

Chronic lateness and/or absenteeism is considered a performance issue and may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

Any employee who is absent without notification, communication or approval of 3 or more consecutive days shall be deemed to have voluntarily resigned from their employment with the Company. At no time will it be deemed a termination of employment on the part of the Company.

### Annual Vacation
In addition to vacation, your time off includes ten (10) paid core holidays, seven (7) paid sick days (any time taken after is unpaid), one (1) paid birthday and the paid Holiday shut down during Christmas and New Year, as illustrated below.

| Years of Service | Paid Vacation Days | Core Holidays | Holiday Shutdown | Birthday | Paid Sick Days | Bereavement |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------------|------------------|----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Any Year | 15 | 10 | 3-5 | 1 | 7 | 3-5 |

If, at the end of the previous calendar year, the employee has worked less than one year, vacation entitlement will be prorated based on time worked from date of hire to 31 December.

Prior approval is needed for time off requests by your manager and must be recorded in our HRIS (BambooHR). If you need 1 week off, you need to send a request at least (1) month in advance. If you need 2 weeks off, you need to send a request at least (2) months in advance and so on.

The employee is required to block out the time in their personal calendar as Out-of-Office and set an automated email response indication. Each of these items should indicate the dates the employee is taking time off and who to contact in the employee's absence should there be an immediate need.

### Sick Leave
Employees are required to submit a medical certificate on the third day of continuous absence due to sickness. Beyond three days and up to the seventh day of continuous absence due to sickness, employees may be put on short-term disability, at management's discretion.

### Pregnancy and Parental Leave
TribalScale's leave top-up benefits supplement your provincial income protection benefits: Employment Insurance (EI). When combined with EI payments, you will receive a total of 80% pay replacement of your base salary for a certain period of time. EI benefits + the additional TribalScale top up is offered for a maximum of 15 weeks. Please note that TribalScale's top up policy applies to the standard parental benefits. If you decide to take the extended parental benefits, TribalScale will prorate your top up payments to equate to the standard parental benefit payout. Employees must successfully apply and be approved for EI in order to receive top-up pay from TribalScale.

#### Leave Types
The distinction between each leave is required for purposes of applying for income protection benefits from Government (eg. under EI) as well as for our tracking and reporting. When applying for EI benefits, you will need to specify which of the following benefits you are applying for:

- Pregnancy (maternity) Leave = available to birth parent
- Still birth (maternity) Leave = available to birth parent
- Adoption Leave = available to adoptive parents
- Parental Leave = available to birth/adoptive parents

#### Leave Length
During pregnancy (maternity), adoption or parental leave, employees who qualify for government EI are entitled to the following: the Birth Parent can take up to 78 consecutive weeks job-protected leave (17 weeks of pregnancy and 61 weeks of parental leave). Non-Birth Parents and/or adoptive parents are eligible for up to 63 consecutive weeks job protected parental leave. In either situation, these benefits begin after a waiting period that is determined by each province (e.g. Ontario has a 2 week unpaid waiting period).

#### Top-Up Pay Eligibility
The below explains TribalScale's top-up pay benefit. TribalScale does not provide any other paid pregnancy (maternity), adoption, parental leave.

- **Pregnancy (Maternity) Leave**: You are eligible to receive a top-up to supplement EI benefits payments for 17 weeks of your maternity leave, up to 80% pay replacement of your base salary.
- **Still Birth (Maternity) Leave**: You are eligible to receive a top-up to supplement EI benefits payments for 12 weeks of your maternity leave, up to 80% pay replacement of your base salary.
- **Adoption Leave**: You are eligible to receive a top-up to supplement EI benefits payments for 4 weeks of your leave, up to 80% pay replacement of your base salary. Adoption leave must be taken concurrently within the first 63 weeks following the adoption of a child.
- **Paternal Leave** (birth mothers are not eligible): You are eligible to receive a top-up to supplement EI benefits for 4 weeks of your leave, up to 80% pay replacement of your base salary. Parental leave must be taken concurrently, it usually follows immediately after maternity or adoption leave, but may be taken any time within 63 weeks of the birth of the child (maternity) or of the date the child comes into your care (adoption).

**Note**: If both parents work for TribalScale the above leaves must be taken at separate times in accordance with EI.

### Jury Duty/Subpoenaed as a Witness
Participation on a jury or acting as a witness are important obligations we all have to our community. When an employee is called for jury duty, or subpoenaed as a witness, he/she is granted the required time off without pay. It is the employee's responsibility to immediately inform his/her Manager and Human Resources when such information is received. In circumstances where the absence of the employee would significantly impact the operations of the department, the employees are requested to cooperate with the Company and the Human Resources department has the right to request that the court excuse the employee from serving on a jury and the employee will be requested to comply with any such Company decision.

### Bereavement Leave
Employees are granted a maximum of three consecutive paid working days off to attend the funeral of an immediate family member which includes spouse, common-law spouse, parent, child, brother, sister, mother/father-in-law, son/daughter-in-law, grandparents or grandchildren. Additional time may be taken as vacation or leave of absence without pay.

### Running for Public Office
Anyone running for municipal, provincial or federal public office is entitled to a leave of absence without pay, provided that the employee is campaigning on his or her own behalf and that the duration of the leave will be one period up to and including the campaigning period.

### Religious Holidays
In recognition of the rich cultural and religious diversity of our employees, an unpaid leave of absence may be granted if the employee observes a religious holiday other than the general holidays listed previously.

### Other Leaves
In addition to the leaves of absence listed above, employees are entitled to all other unpaid leaves of absence required by law which include: Family Medical Leave, Organ Donor Leave, Family Caregiver Leave, Critically Ill Child Care Leave, Crime-Related Child Death or Disappearance Leave, Reservist Leave and Emergency Leave for Declared Emergencies.

## Chapter 4: Salary Administration

### Overview
Our salary policy is designed to attract, retain and motivate highly qualified employees. We review our compensation structures on a regular basis to ensure the competitive edge over other comparable industry-leading companies.

### Salary Disbursements
There are 24 pay periods in a year. A payroll disbursement calendar is published annually for employees information. Salaries are deposited directly into the employee's bank account as a preferred salary disbursement procedure. However, employees may opt to receive cheques instead.

### Salary Administration

#### Approach to Pay
The approach that guides the development and management of pay policies and programs for employees of TribalScale is:

- All employees have the opportunity to earn competitive pay
- The total pay that each employee earns reflects the individual's contribution to the Corporation's business success
- Employees have access to information about the pay policies that apply to them
- Pay policies and programs reflect the principles of pay equity

#### The Manager's Role
Managers work with employees to establish performance and development objectives. They coach and develop individual employees to continually enhance performance and ensure the success of TribalScale.

Managers and Human Resources determine an appropriate level for each employee's base pay. This level is monitored and assessed to ensure that it continues to be consistent with employee performance and development. Managers also have a responsibility to manage a salary budget and provide appropriate increases to the employee's base pay, as approved by Human Resources.

#### Salary Reviews
##### Introduction
An important management responsibility is to keep the salary structure, within permissible constraints, toward the identified target position. This is done at regular intervals in accordance with the job market and permitted budgets.

Managers are responsible for ensuring that the employee's target pay position continues to be appropriate given his/her performance. Managers are responsible for monitoring employee performance throughout the year as part of this ongoing process.

##### Merit Increases
Merit increases are awarded based on demonstrated employee performance and are independent of salary increases related to promotional moves or job re-evaluation.

##### Authorization and Responsibility
The appropriate management level must authorize all salary actions:
- The immediate supervisor
- CEO, COO or Director of Human Resources

Executive Compensation is determined and maintained by the Board of Directors. Any increases/changes to the Executive Compensation requires the approval of the Board of Directors.

Managers must secure all necessary authorizations before informing employees of salary changes.

##### Considerations for Granting Pay Increases
Listed below are examples of employees whose situations require careful consideration before a base pay increase is granted:

- Where performance is rated as "not meeting expectations"
- Where performance has not been rated. A recommendation for a base pay increase would be on an exception basis
- Where formal remedial discipline has taken place. Managers should review each situation with Human Resources
- Where employees who have confirmed resignations pending (excluding retirements)
- New hires to the Corporation with less than 12 months service would typically not have a performance rating

## Chapter 5: Company Travel and Business Expenses

### Overview
Employees may be reimbursed for reasonable business expenses. If an employee is required to travel on company business, he/she should, upon completion of the trip, submit an expense report. The Corporation will reimburse all reasonable expenses properly incurred in connection with TribalScale's business requirements.

More detailed information is contained in TribalScale Inc.'s Travel & Expense Policy document, observance of which is required by all employees.

### Expense Reporting and Reimbursement Guidelines
TribalScale recognizes that from time to time an employee may incur out-of-pocket expenses in the performance of their duties. TribalScale will only reimburse those expenses that are pre-approved, properly documented, supported and deemed necessary and reasonable in the performance of the employee's duties. TribalScale reserves full discretion in making this determination.

Employees seeking reimbursement are required to complete and submit an expense report through Expensify, our expense management system, making sure to attach all valid and original itemized receipts. The expense report must be submitted no later than 30 days after the month-end in which the expense is incurred.
