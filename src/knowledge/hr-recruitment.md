
# Recruitment Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
TribalScale Inc. aims to employ candidates possessing the required qualifications for a position. This policy outlines the procedures followed when qualified internal candidates are not available and external candidates are considered, when internal candidates are hired, and when search firms are utilized.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All recruitment and hiring activities for regular full-time, part-time, and intern/co-op positions
- Internal and external candidate recruitment processes
- Pre-employment screening and background checks
- Onboarding and orientation of new employees
- Transfers and rehires within TribalScale
- Use of search firms and recruitment agencies

### 1.3 Target Audience
- Hiring managers and departmental leaders
- Human Resources personnel and recruiters
- External candidates and applicants
- Search firms and recruitment agencies
- Employees involved in interview and selection processes

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Recruitment Process
TribalScale Inc. aims to employ candidates possessing the required qualifications for a position. This policy outlines procedures for external candidate consideration when qualified internal candidates are not available, internal hiring processes, and utilization of search firms.

### 3.2 Position Requisitions
Hiring Officials use their departmental processes for gaining general and budgetary approval for creating and re-filling positions. Requisitions are entered by the hiring manager or delegate into the recruitment system, routed for approval, and reviewed by a recruiter prior to posting.

### 3.3 Job Postings
Hiring of regular Full-time, Part-time or Intern/CO-OP positions is coordinated through Human Resources. All external postings are posted for a minimum of ten business days and viewable to internal and/or external applicants on the career tab of the TribalScale Inc. website and external job hunt websites.

### 3.4 Advertising and Outreach
External advertising for open positions at TribalScale Inc. is coordinated through Human Resources. This includes, but is not limited to, advertising on websites, targeted recruitment sites, and additional means.

### 3.5 Search Firms
While the use of outside search firms is discouraged, Human Resources can review and recommend firms and is responsible to secure contracts on behalf of TribalScale Inc. when needed. All search firms doing business with TribalScale Inc. must contractually agree to comply with company policies.

### 3.6 Applications
All applicants, excluding certain instances of internal hiring or referrals, complete an online application when applying for open positions.

### 3.7 Candidate Pools and Diversity
To ensure consistency in the hiring processes and adherence to compliance requirements, Human Resources complies with and reviews the hiring in accordance to equality, diversity and inclusion to determine if specific hiring goals have been established for women and/or minorities.

## 4. Due Diligence Procedures

### 4.1 Screening and Testing
The Recruiter will pre-screen candidates before booking interviews to be carried out alongside Hiring Officers. Use of testing tools are reviewed on an ongoing basis by Human Resources.

### 4.2 Interview Process
The Hiring Official begins with a phone interview, followed by in-person interviews with qualified candidates. A consistent line of inquiry is used to determine selection of the final candidate(s). Interview questions are designed to be job-related and not to be or perceived to be about race, sex, national or ethnic origin, religion, marital or parental status, sexual orientation, gender identity or gender expression, disability or genetic information.

### 4.3 Background Checks
TribalScale may conduct pre-employment background checks or conduct a background check during employment upon client request. Where pre-employment background checks are used, candidates should be informed upon reviewing the job posting. All offers of employment outline that the offer and employment is conditional upon the successful completion of the background check. Human Resources utilizes a third party vendor to conduct all pre-employment background checks.

### 4.4 Background Check Guidelines
These guidelines apply to the evaluation of background check results:
- Employment eligibility of new hires, transfers, and rehires will be determined by reviewing the findings against established criteria and the job duties/requirements for each position. The determination will take into account the position applied for, as well as the relevant factors, including any legal and/or regulatory requirements
- If the results received contradict the application, the candidate will be removed from the process due to falsification of application
- If a TribalScale employee seeking a transfer or promotion does not qualify for the new position based on the results of the background check, he/she will no longer be considered for the new position, subject to Board Approval

### 4.5 Document Retention
Hiring Managers are required to keep any notes, resumes, electronic (emails) or paper documents used during the interview process for two years after the hire is made. For purposes of appropriate record-keeping, and to comply with TribalScale's Document Retention Policy, the Hiring Manager will provide justification for the decision made for each candidate referred for an interview.

## 5. Hiring Procedures

### 5.1 Employment Offers
When the Hiring Official has made the final selection of the candidate to hire, the department may prepare an offer of employment letter with legal consultation, if needed. Once accepted, Human Resources will begin to prepare for the onboarding process.

### 5.2 Conditional Offers
Under certain circumstances, it may be necessary to rescind a conditional offer of employment. These circumstances include, but are not limited to the following:
- A candidate fails to comply with established timelines associated with the pre-employment screening process
- A candidate does not pass the pre-employment screen
- A candidate omits or falsified information on the application or related documents
- Reference information is unfavorable or inconsistent with information provided by the candidate

In the event that it is appropriate to rescind an offer, Human Resources will notify the Hiring Officer as well as the candidate in writing.

### 5.3 Work Eligibility
New hires will be required to review and sign all onboarding documents, including but not limited to the Acceptable Use Policy for Electronic Resources and the Proprietary Information and Inventions Agreement (PIIA), by no later than the first day of employment.

Documentation that establishes eligibility to work in Canada or the United States of America depending on the primary work site, must be presented before relocation. Hiring Officials who are considering hiring a foreign national applicant will maintain contact with immigration service providers to make sure that all legal and policy requirements are met before any job offer is made.

### 5.4 Rehires
A former employee may be considered for rehire if the separation from prior employment was voluntary and appropriate notice was provided and if the employee worked through the notice period as required by the department. The notice period is two weeks in duration and initiated by a written notice.

The Recruiter will review the personnel file of the former employee who has applied for reemployment and verify eligibility for rehire before forwarding the application. The Hiring Manager will be advised of the former employee's eligibility for rehire and the requirement to check references.

### 5.5 Transfers
Employees must have successfully completed the onboarding period and remained in their current position for a minimum six month evaluation period to be eligible for a transfer.

## 6. Onboarding Process

### 6.1 Onboarding Initiation
The onboarding process begins with emailed information from the HR Coordinator, five business days before the start date. On the first day of work, new hires are greeted upon arrival, shown to their workspace and asked to join an onboarding presentation which concludes with a tour of the office facility.

### 6.2 New Employee Orientation Period
TribalScale utilizes the three month probationary period to provide new employees the opportunity to acclimate to their new position, assess the skills and provide feedback where needed. Check-in meetings are conducted on the 30, 60, and 90 day mark to ensure a smooth transition. Internal transfers and promotions are not subject to an orientation period.

### 6.3 Performance During Orientation
In the event job performance or conduct during orientation is concerning or unsatisfactory; supervisors should promptly provide direction to the new employee. If the concerns continue, the supervisor should give the employee written notice of the deficiency and explain how the performance or conduct needs to improve in order to continue employment. During the orientation period, employment may be terminated without notice. However, termination of employment should be reviewed with Human Resources prior to taking action.

### 6.4 Employee Resignation
If an employee feels that the employment opportunity is not meeting their needs, they are not required to provide a two week resignation notice.

## 7. Responsibilities

### 7.1 Hiring Managers
- Complete requisitions with appropriate approvals and budget authorization
- Participate in interview process and candidate selection
- Ensure compliance with interview guidelines and equal opportunity requirements
- Maintain interview documentation for required retention period
- Provide justification for hiring decisions
- Conduct performance check-ins during probationary period

### 7.2 Human Resources
- Coordinate recruitment process and job postings
- Review and approve use of search firms
- Conduct pre-screening and coordinate interviews
- Manage background check process through third-party vendor
- Prepare employment offers and onboarding materials
- Ensure compliance with legal and regulatory requirements
- Maintain recruitment documentation retention

### 7.3 Recruiters
- Review requisitions prior to posting
- Pre-screen candidates before interviews
- Coordinate interview schedules with hiring managers
- Review candidate pools for diversity and inclusion goals
- Assist with background check evaluations
- Verify rehire eligibility for former employees

### 7.4 Search Firms
- Comply with all TribalScale policies and procedures
- Maintain confidentiality of recruitment processes
- Provide qualified candidates that meet position requirements
- Adhere to contractual agreements with TribalScale

## 8. Compliance and Enforcement

### 8.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- Regular review of recruitment processes for compliance
- Documentation of hiring decisions and justifications
- Audit of background check procedures
- Review of diversity and inclusion in hiring outcomes
- Annual assessment of recruitment effectiveness

### 8.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- **Policy Violations**: Verbal warning and corrective action
- **Falsification of Application**: Immediate removal from process
- **Discriminatory Practices**: Investigation and disciplinary action
- **Document Retention Violations**: Written warning and remediation
- **Unauthorized Hiring**: Reversal of hiring decision

### 8.3 Exceptions
Exceptions require:
- HR approval for non-standard recruitment processes
- Legal consultation for complex hiring situations
- Senior management approval for policy deviations
- Documentation of business justification
- Regular monitoring of exception validity
