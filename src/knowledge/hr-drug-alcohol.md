
# Drug and Alcohol Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
The employees of TribalScale are our most valuable resource, and for that reason, their health and safety is of paramount concern. TribalScale has adopted this policy to communicate its expectations and guidelines surrounding recreational alcohol and drug use, misuse, and abuse.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All TribalScale employees, contractors, and temporary workers
- All workplace locations and company-sponsored events
- Use of alcohol and controlled substances (including legalized recreational substances)
- Prescription medications (including medical marijuana)
- Both on-site and off-site work activities
- Work performed during TribalScale's listed office hours

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All employees and contractors
- Managers and supervisors
- HR personnel
- Safety and security teams
- Medical and EAP providers

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|

| Workplace Safety Act | Provincial legislation ensuring workplace health and safety |
| Duty to Accommodate | Legal obligation to provide reasonable accommodations for disabilities |

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Alcohol Consumption Guidelines
- TribalScale strictly prohibits the consumption of alcohol during working hours (9am-5pm)
- Exception for reasonable consumption during client engagements, networking events, company events, and Friday social
- Reasonable consumption pertains to understanding of company representation upon alcohol consumption
- Alcohol consumption must not impair work performance or safety

### 3.2 Legalized Recreational Substances
- Use of legalized recreational substances is strictly prohibited during work hours
- Exception for client meetings, company events, and networking events with reasonable limits
- Employees are strictly prohibited from reporting to work while under the influence
- If under the influence, employee must refuse reporting to work (not entitled to unexcused absences)
- Sale and/or distribution of legalized substances is strictly prohibited in the workplace

### 3.3 Medical Marijuana Policy
- TribalScale has a duty to accommodate an employee's medical need for marijuana
- Employee is not entitled to be impaired in the workplace due to medical marijuana use
- Employee is not entitled to smoke in the workplace
- Employee is not entitled to develop unexcused late arrivals or absences
- If medical use causes impairment or side effects, communicate through Accommodation Policy
- Provide allowance amount for marijuana use and abide by Accommodation Policy procedures

### 3.4 Workplace Impairment Standards
- Employees must arrive to work fit for duty and able to perform duties safely and to expected standards
- Employees must remain fit for duty for the duration of TribalScale's listed office hours
- No impairment during work hours or company activities
- Employees under the influence pose serious health and safety risks

### 3.5 Substance Possession and Distribution
- Possession, distribution, or sale of drugs or alcohol during work hours is strictly prohibited
- This includes during breaks and on company property
- Applies to both on-site and off-site work activities
- Employees must avoid consumption, possession, sale, or distribution of non-medical substances

### 3.6 Accommodation and Support
- If employee requires accommodations for addiction or dependency, refer to Accommodation Policy
- Employee will not be disciplined for seeking help under reasonable grounds
- Management must identify situations that may cause concern regarding employee's ability to perform safely
- Maintain confidentiality and employee privacy
- Attempt to find suitable workplace accommodation for employees with medical marijuana prescriptions

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 All Employees
- Arrive to work fit for duty, and remain fit for duty throughout the work day
- Perform work safely in accordance with company-established safe work practices
- Avoid the consumption, possession, sale, or distribution of non-medical marijuana, other drugs, or alcohol on company property, and during working hours even if off company property (with exceptions listed above)
- When off duty, refuse a request to come into work if unfit for duty
- Report limitations and required modifications as a result of medically approved marijuana use
- Report unfit co-workers to management
- Seek advice or appropriate treatment, where required
- Communicate dependency or emerging dependency in order to apply for accommodation under the company's duty to accommodate
- Follow the after-care program, where established
- Abide by all governing legislation pertaining to the possession and use of legalized marijuana
- Inform employer of any addictions and/or abuse through Accommodation Policy

### 4.2 Managers and Supervisors
- Identify any situations that may cause concern regarding an employee's ability to safely perform their job functions
- Ensure that any employee who asks for help due to a drug or alcohol dependency is provided with the appropriate support (including accommodation) and is not disciplined for doing so, abiding by the duty to accommodate
- Maintain confidentiality and employee privacy
- Attempt to find suitable workplace accommodation for disabled employees who have a prescription for medical marijuana use, just as would be required for any other disabled employee with a medical drug prescription

### 4.3 HR Department
- Administer drug and alcohol policy and procedures
- Provide guidance on accommodation for substance-related disabilities
- Ensure compliance with Bill C-45, Workplace Safety Act, and human rights legislation
- Coordinate with medical professionals for accommodation assessments
- Maintain confidential records of accommodation requests and medical information

### 4.4 Company Leadership
- Communicate expectations surrounding drug and alcohol use, misuse, and abuse
- Support accommodation requests for employees with substance-related disabilities
- Ensure workplace safety and health standards are maintained
- Provide resources for employee assistance and treatment programs

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 Accommodation Request Process
1. Employee identifies need for accommodation due to drug/alcohol dependency or medical marijuana use
2. Employee submits request through Accommodation Policy procedures
3. HR reviews request and provides appropriate support
4. Employee is not disciplined for seeking help under reasonable grounds
5. Accommodation plan developed in partnership with employee, manager, and HR

### 5.2 Medical Marijuana Accommodation
1. Employee provides prescription and allowance amount for medical marijuana use
2. Employee abides by Accommodation Policy procedures
3. HR assesses workplace safety implications
4. Reasonable accommodations provided (schedule modifications, etc.)
5. Employee agrees not to be impaired, smoke in workplace, or develop unexcused absences

### 5.3 Reporting Unfit Co-workers
1. Employee observes co-worker who appears unfit for duty
2. Employee reports concerns to management confidentially
3. Management investigates the situation discreetly
4. Appropriate action taken based on findings
5. Confidentiality maintained throughout process

### 5.4 Treatment and Support Seeking
1. Employee recognizes need for assistance with substance use
2. Employee communicates with manager or HR about dependency
3. Employee directed to appropriate support resources
4. Accommodation provided under duty to accommodate
5. Employee not disciplined for seeking help in good faith

### 5.5 After-Care Program Compliance
1. Employee completes treatment or rehabilitation program
2. After-care program established with specific requirements
3. Employee follows program guidelines and reporting requirements
4. Regular check-ins with manager and HR
5. Support provided to maintain sobriety and workplace performance

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- Regular training on drug and alcohol policy
- Testing program compliance monitoring
- Incident and violation trend analysis
- Annual program effectiveness review
- Regulatory compliance audits

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
Employees found in violation of this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment. Where applicable, TribalScale may also take legal action in accordance with the law.

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions require:
- Medical necessity documentation
- Legal compliance verification
- Safety assessment approval
- Senior management approval
- Regular monitoring requirements
