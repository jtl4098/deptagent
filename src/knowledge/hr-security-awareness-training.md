
# Security Awareness Training Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes requirements for security awareness training and education programs at TribalScale. It ensures all employees, contractors, and third parties understand their security responsibilities and can identify and respond to security threats.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary workers
- Third-party service providers with access to TribalScale systems
- Security awareness training content and delivery methods
- Phishing awareness and simulation programs
- Annual security training requirements
- Training effectiveness measurement and reporting

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All TribalScale personnel
- Human Resources and training coordinators
- Information Security team
- Management and supervisors
- Third-party service providers

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Security Awareness | Knowledge and understanding of information security principles and practices |
| Phishing Simulation | Controlled exercise to test employee response to phishing attempts |
| Social Engineering | Manipulation of people to divulge confidential information |
| Security Incident | Any event that compromises or threatens to compromise information security |
| Training Effectiveness | Measurement of how well training achieves its security objectives |

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Training Requirements
All personnel must complete security awareness training:
- **New Hires**: Security training within first week of employment
- **Annual Training**: All employees complete annual security refresher
- **Role-Specific Training**: Additional training based on job responsibilities
- **Third Parties**: Contractors and vendors complete applicable training
- **Updates**: Training for significant policy changes or new threats

### 3.2 Training Content
Security awareness training must cover:
- **Basic Security Principles**: Confidentiality, integrity, and availability
- **Password Security**: Strong passwords, multi-factor authentication
- **Phishing Awareness**: Recognizing and reporting suspicious emails
- **Physical Security**: Workplace security and clean desk policies
- **Data Handling**: Proper handling of sensitive information
- **Incident Reporting**: Procedures for reporting security concerns
- **Remote Work Security**: Secure practices for remote and mobile work

### 3.3 Phishing Awareness Program
TribalScale maintains an active phishing awareness program:
- **Regular Simulations**: Quarterly phishing simulation exercises
- **Real-Time Training**: Immediate feedback for simulation participants
- **Reporting Procedures**: Clear instructions for reporting suspicious emails
- **Metrics Tracking**: Monitoring of reporting rates and click rates
- **Remediation Training**: Additional training for individuals who fail simulations

### 3.4 Training Delivery Methods
Training is delivered through multiple channels:
- **Online Modules**: Self-paced e-learning courses
- **Live Sessions**: Instructor-led workshops and presentations
- **Simulations**: Interactive phishing and security exercises
- **Just-in-Time Training**: Targeted training for specific threats
- **Refresher Content**: Short monthly security awareness communications

### 3.5 Training Effectiveness
Training programs include effectiveness measures:
- **Knowledge Assessments**: Pre and post-training quizzes
- **Behavioral Metrics**: Phishing simulation success rates
- **Incident Trends**: Correlation between training and security incidents
- **Feedback Surveys**: Participant feedback on training quality
- **Compliance Tracking**: Completion rates and certification status

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Human Resources
- Oversee security awareness training program
- Coordinate training scheduling and delivery
- Track training completion and compliance
- Maintain training records and certifications
- Support onboarding training integration

### 4.2 Information Security Team
- Develop and maintain training content
- Conduct phishing simulations and exercises
- Monitor training effectiveness and metrics
- Provide subject matter expertise for training
- Respond to training-related security incidents

### 4.3 Managers and Supervisors
- Ensure their team members complete required training
- Reinforce security awareness in team meetings
- Address security concerns raised by team members
- Model appropriate security behaviors
- Participate in management-specific security training

### 4.4 All Employees
- Complete all assigned security training
- Apply security knowledge in daily work activities
- Report suspicious activities and security concerns
- Participate in phishing simulations and exercises
- Stay current with evolving security threats

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 New Hire Training Process
1. HR coordinates security training during onboarding
2. New hire completes online security awareness module
3. Review of key policies and procedures
4. Acknowledgment of security responsibilities
5. Follow-up training scheduled as needed

### 5.2 Annual Training Process
1. HR distributes annual training calendar
2. Employees complete required training modules
3. Knowledge assessments administered
4. Certificates issued upon completion
5. Non-compliance tracked and addressed

### 5.3 Phishing Simulation Process
1. Security team plans simulation exercise
2. Simulated phishing emails sent to participants
3. Immediate feedback provided to participants
4. Detailed results analyzed and reported
5. Remediation training provided as needed

### 5.4 Training Effectiveness Assessment
1. Collect training completion and assessment data
2. Analyze phishing simulation results
3. Review security incident trends
4. Gather participant feedback
5. Report findings to executive leadership

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Compliance Monitoring
- Regular review of training completion rates
- Analysis of phishing simulation results
- Monitoring of security incident reporting trends
- Assessment of training content relevance and effectiveness

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- Failure to complete training: Restricted system access until completion
- Poor phishing simulation performance: Mandatory remediation training
- Repeated non-compliance: Performance improvement plan
- Security policy violations: Standard disciplinary procedures

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions to training requirements require:
- Documented business justification
- Risk assessment of the exception
- Approval from Human Resources and Information Security
- Implementation of compensating security measures
- Regular review of exception validity
