
# Time Off Policy - Canada

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This time off policy provides you flexibility when scheduling your time off. Employee time off, consists of vacation, holidays, birthday and sick time.

In addition to vacation, your time off includes ten (10) paid core holidays, seven(7) paid sick days (anytime taken after is unpaid), one (1) paid birthday and the paid Holiday shut down during Christmas and New Year, as illustrated below.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees in Canadian provinces and territories
- Regular employees working at least 30 hours per week
- All locations and remote work arrangements
- Federal and provincial employment standards (Bill 168, Bill 47, Bill 148)
- Both union and non-union employees
- All types of paid and unpaid leave including vacation, holidays, sick time, and special leaves

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All Canada-based employees
- Managers and supervisors
- HR personnel
- Payroll administrators
- Legal and compliance teams

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Eligibility Requirements
Employees must work at least 30 hours per week to be eligible for vacation time off work. If you are a regular employee working at least 30 hours per week, you are eligible for vacation, holidays, and sick time and will begin to accrue vacation on your date of hire. If you work under 30 hours per week, you will receive vacation pay at a rate of 6% of time worked. You begin to accrue vacation time on your date of hire. If you have any questions please reach out to hr@tribalscale.com.

### 3.2 Statutory Paid Company Holidays
Take time with family and friends to celebrate TribalScale's core holidays. Where a Holiday falls on a weekend, the next working day is usually considered that holiday.

| Holiday | Date |
|---------|------|
| New Years Day | Always January 1st |
| Family Day | Third Monday in February |
| Good Friday | Friday before Easter Sunday |
| Victoria Day | Third Monday of May |
| Canada Day | Always July 1st |
| Civic Holiday | First Monday of August |
| Labour Day | First Monday in September |
| Thanksgiving Day | Second Monday in October |
| Christmas Day | Always December 25th |
| Boxing Day | Always December 26th |

### 3.3 Holiday Shut Down
TribalScale provides paid Holiday shut down during Christmas and New Year, typically 3-5 days depending on the year and business requirements.

### 3.4 Annual Vacation Carryover
On January 1st of a New Year, for any earned but unused vacation time, in your vacation bank, you will automatically carry over up to 5 days vacation time, from one calendar year to the next. Any unused vacation time in excess of 5 days, cannot be carried over and is forfeited, with the exceptions noted below.

| Province | Annual Vacation Carryover Maximum |
|----------|-----------------------------------|
| Ontario | All Years = 2 weeks |
| Alberta, British Columbia, Manitoba, Quebec | Years 0-4 = 2 weeks, Years 5+ = 3 weeks |
| New Brunswick, Nova Scotia | Years 0-7 = 2 weeks, Years 8+ = 3 weeks |

### 3.5 Vacation Upon Termination
Subject to the terms of this policy, TribalScale will pay for any accrued unused vacation days as of your date of termination. Any other time off is not eligible for payment.

### 3.6 Time Off Requests
Prior approval by your manager for taking any time off (e.g., vacation, sick day, birthday, etc) is required and must be recorded in BambooHR. If you need 1 week off, you need to send a request at least (1) month in advance. If you need 2 weeks off, you need to send a request at least (2) months in advance and so on.

### 3.7 Birthday Time Off
Employees are eligible for one (1) day off per calendar year on their birthday. Please request your birthday off at least (2) weeks in advance through BambooHR. Birthday time off is paid at your regular base rate of pay. This time is not to be used as vacation time. If your birthday falls on a weekend, speak to your manager to get another day off. It will be up to the manager's discretion to approve a different day off, instead your actual birthday.

### 3.8 Sick Time
Employees are eligible for unlimited sick days, up to 7 days employer paid per calendar year. After the 7 days, any additional time off requested as sick time will be unpaid. Time off includes illness, hospitalization, and doctor appointments. Sick time is paid at your regular base rate of pay. This time is not to be used as vacation time. Sick time cannot be payout or carried forward at the end of year.

### 3.9 Bereavement Leave
TribalScale provides paid time off in the event of the death of specified family members. An employee is entitled up to five (5) consecutive working days of paid leave for immediate family and up to three (3) consecutive working days of paid leave for non-immediate family. Bereavement leave must be entered in BambooHR under Bereavement.

**Immediate Family (up to 5 consecutive work days):**
- Spouse, Qualified Domestic Partner
- Parent, Parent-in-law, Step-parent
- Sibling
- Brother-/Sister-in-law
- Child, Stepchild, Foster Child

**Non-Immediate Family (up to 3 consecutive work days):**
- Grandparent, Grandparent-in-law, Grandchildren
- Ex-spouse
- Aunt, Uncle
- Niece, Nephew
- Foster parent

*If additional time off is required employees should work with their managers and HR.

### 3.10 Additional Leaves

**Family Responsibility Leave:**
In accordance with the passing of Bill 47, TribalScale provides job protected unpaid time off of up to 28 weeks. This leave may be taken to provide care and support to family members in the event of medical emergencies. Care and support includes: providing psychological or emotional support, arranging for care by a third party provider or directly providing or participating in the care of the family member.

**Child Death Leave:**
In accordance to the passing of Bill 148, TribalScale provides job protected unpaid time off in the event of the death of your child. This job protected leave is up to 104 weeks and this leave can be taken after Bereavement leave.

**Crime Related Child Disappearance Leave:**
In accordance to the passing of Bill 148, TribalScale provides job protected time off in the event of a crime related disappearance of your child. This job protected leave is up to 104 weeks of unpaid time off.

**Domestic and/or Sexual Violence Leave:**
In accordance to the passing of Bill 148, TribalScale provides you up to 15 weeks of job protected unpaid leave. This leave is available to obtain specific treatment/counseling/protection. This leave is also available to an employee who is the subject of such violence or parent of a child who is subject to such violence.

**Pregnancy (Maternity) and Parental Leave:**
In accordance to the passing of Bill 148, TribalScale has updated its Pregnancy(maternity) and Parental Leave policy in accordance to Bill 148. Please see the full policy titled "TribalScale Pregnancy (Maternity) and Parental Leave Policy - Canada," which can be found in the TribalScale Wiki under Policies.

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Employees
- Provide reasonable notice for time off requests
- Submit required medical documentation
- Communicate coverage arrangements
- Return to work as scheduled
- Maintain contact during leaves

### 4.2 Managers and Supervisors
- Approve time off based on operational requirements
- Ensure adequate coverage during absences
- Support employees during leave
- Monitor return-to-work progress
- Address performance issues related to leave

### 4.3 HR Department
- Administer leave programs by jurisdiction
- Ensure compliance with provincial employment standards
- Process leave payments and benefits
- Provide guidance on leave entitlements
- Maintain accurate leave records

### 4.4 Payroll Department
- Calculate vacation pay and holiday pay accurately
- Process leave payments according to regulations
- Track vacation accruals and usage
- Coordinate with benefits during unpaid leaves
- Report statutory requirements compliance

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 Vacation Request Process
1. Employee submits vacation request with dates
2. Manager reviews for business impact and coverage
3. Approval or alternative dates suggested
4. Vacation entered into scheduling system
5. Payroll notification for vacation pay

### 5.2 Parental Leave Process
1. Employee notifies HR of pregnancy/birth plans
2. HR provides EI forms and leave information
3. Leave dates confirmed with manager
4. Benefits coordination arranged
5. Return-to-work planning initiated

### 5.3 Statutory Holiday Scheduling
1. Company holiday schedule published annually
2. Alternative arrangements for shift workers
3. Overtime procedures for holiday work
4. Religious accommodation requests handled
5. Holiday pay calculations verified

### 5.4 Return from Medical Leave
1. Employee provides medical clearance
2. Gradual return-to-work plan developed if needed
3. Accommodations implemented as required
4. Performance monitoring during transition
5. Follow-up support provided

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- Regular compliance audits with provincial standards
- Vacation pay calculations verified quarterly
- Leave utilization reporting by jurisdiction
- Annual policy review for regulatory changes
- Employee feedback on leave administration

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- **Administrative Errors**: Correction and staff training
- **Policy Violations**: Verbal warning and retraining
- **ESA Violations**: Legal penalties and back pay
- **Abuse of Leave**: Written warning and leave restrictions
- **Repeated Violations**: Progressive disciplinary action

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions require:
- Provincial law compliance verification
- Senior management approval
- Legal consultation when applicable
- Documentation of business justification
- Regular monitoring and reassessment
