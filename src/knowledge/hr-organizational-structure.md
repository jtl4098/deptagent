
# Organizational Structure Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
This policy documents TribalScale's organizational structure, including staffing levels, team compositions, and reporting relationships. It ensures transparency in organizational operations and supports compliance with regulatory requirements for staffing disclosures.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- Current organizational structure and staffing levels
- Team composition and roles
- Reporting relationships and responsibilities
- Changes to organizational structure
- Client and regulatory communications regarding staffing

### 1.3 Target Audience
- All employees and contractors
- Clients and prospective clients
- Regulatory bodies and auditors
- Investors and stakeholders
- Human Resources and management teams

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Organizational Structure | Formal arrangement of roles, responsibilities, and reporting relationships |
| Staffing Level | Total number of employees or contractors in specific roles or departments |
| Team Composition | Breakdown of roles and responsibilities within a team |
| Reporting Relationship | Hierarchical connection between positions and their supervisors |
| FTE | Full-Time Equivalent - standard measure of employee workload |

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Overall Staffing
TribalScale maintains a total workforce of 30 employees, distributed across various functional areas to support our technology consulting and development services.

### 3.2 IT and Technical Staff
TribalScale employs 1 dedicated IT Manager who oversees all technology infrastructure, systems administration, and technical support functions.

### 3.3 Information Security Staff
TribalScale has 3 team members who manage Information Security as part of their role responsibilities. These individuals are full-time TribalScale employees who integrate information security practices into their primary job functions.

### 3.4 Team Structure
The organizational structure supports a collaborative, cross-functional approach where information security responsibilities are distributed across relevant teams rather than maintained as a separate, dedicated security department.

### 3.5 Staffing Changes
Any changes to organizational structure or staffing levels must be:
- Approved by executive leadership
- Documented in organizational records
- Communicated to relevant stakeholders
- Updated in this policy document

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Executive Leadership
- Approve organizational structure changes
- Ensure adequate staffing for business needs
- Maintain oversight of key positions and roles

### 4.2 Human Resources
- Maintain accurate records of organizational structure
- Track staffing levels and changes
- Support recruitment for critical positions
- Ensure compliance with employment regulations

### 4.3 Department Managers
- Maintain current team compositions
- Report staffing changes to HR
- Ensure proper role coverage and responsibilities
- Participate in organizational planning

### 4.4 Employees
- Understand their role in the organizational structure
- Maintain awareness of reporting relationships
- Participate in organizational change processes

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 Organizational Structure Updates
1. Identify need for organizational change
2. Conduct impact assessment and planning
3. Obtain executive approval
4. Implement changes with proper communication
5. Update documentation and records
6. Monitor and adjust as needed

### 5.2 Staffing Level Reporting
1. Maintain current staffing records
2. Conduct quarterly staffing reviews
3. Report significant changes to leadership
4. Update client communications as needed
5. Ensure regulatory compliance

### 5.3 Role Documentation
1. Document all positions and responsibilities
2. Maintain job descriptions and requirements
3. Update role information as changes occur
4. Ensure proper approval chains are documented

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Compliance Monitoring
- Regular review of organizational structure accuracy
- Verification of staffing level records
- Audit of role and responsibility documentation
- Compliance with regulatory reporting requirements

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- Inaccurate staffing disclosures: Corrective action and re-disclosure
- Unauthorized organizational changes: Reversal of changes
- Failure to maintain records: Written warning and remediation

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions to organizational structure changes require:
- Business justification documentation
- Executive leadership approval
- HR and legal review
- Risk assessment of the proposed change
- Monitoring and review of exception validity
