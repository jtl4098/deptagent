
# Work From Home Policy

## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose
Compliance with the Work From Home Policy does not alter your duties, obligations, responsibilities and/or conditions of employment with TribalScale, unless specifically agreed upon in writing. TribalScale may end the remote work assignment at any time with or without cause, and use of the Work From Home Policy is subject to the following conditions.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All TribalScale employees approved for work from home arrangements
- Work performed in designated home workspaces
- Use of personal devices and home networks for work
- All locations where work from home is conducted
- Both full-time and temporary work from home arrangements

### 1.3 Target Audience
- Employees participating in remote work programs
- Managers supervising remote employees
- IT and security teams supporting remote work
- HR team administering remote work agreements
- Facilities and procurement teams

## 2. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Work From Home | Work performed in a designated home workspace instead of traditional office premises |
| Designated Workspace | Dedicated space in employee's home (office, spare bedroom, etc.) used exclusively for work |
| Designated Work Time | Regular assigned work schedule (9:00am-5:00pm) unless otherwise approved by manager |
| Remote Meetings | Meetings attended via video conference or phone while working from home |
| BambooHR | TribalScale's HR management system for time off requests and approvals |
| FMLA | Family and Medical Leave Act - federal law providing job protection for family/medical leave |

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Designated Work Time
Employees must follow their regular assigned work schedule 9:00am-5:00pm, unless otherwise discussed with and approved by the employee's manager. All arrangements for childcare, elder care, etc. must be arranged so as not to interfere with the employee's regular work schedule.

### 3.2 Designated Workspace
It is the responsibility of the employee to designate a remote workspace, which is typically a space in the employee's home (an office, spare bedroom, etc.). The designated workspace must be kept in a safe condition, free from hazards to both the employee and the equipment.

Should the employee sustain any injuries in their designated workspace and in conjunction with his or her regular work duties, the employee is responsible for notifying his or her manager of such injuries as soon as is practicable. TribalScale will not be responsible for any injuries to the employee or any third parties outside of the designated workspace or during the employee's non-working time.

### 3.3 Rules and Policies
All TribalScale's policies, including those set forth in the Employee Handbook, apply while working from a designated workspace. These policies include, but are not limited to, policies regarding attendance, confidentiality, and policies prohibiting harassment.

Employees are reminded that this Work From Home Policy is not to be used in place of sick leave, FMLA leave, etc. If employees are on sick leave or away on vacation, please refer to TribalScale's Time Off Policy and request time off accordingly through appointed HRIS (BambooHR) and manager approval.

### 3.4 Remote Meetings
Employees working from home are still expected to attend all essential meetings via video conference or by phone.

### 3.5 Confidentiality and Security
Employees working remotely are reminded that even if they are working from a designated workspace, they are bound by any confidentiality and/or security agreements they signed in connection with their employment with TribalScale, and any confidentiality and/or security policies contained in the Employee Handbook.

Thus, consistent with TribalScale's expectations of information security for employees working in the office, remote employees will be expected to ensure the protection of proprietary company and customer information accessible from their home office. Steps include regular password maintenance, locked file cabinets and desks to store sensitive information, and any other measures appropriate for the job and the environment of the designated workspace.

### 3.6 Policy Termination
TribalScale may end the remote work assignment at any time with or without cause.

### 3.7 Questions
Any questions regarding this Work From Home Policy may be directed to your manager or to HR.

## 4. Responsibilities

### 4.1 Remote Employees
- Maintain productivity and meet performance expectations
- Ensure secure work environment and practices
- Communicate regularly with manager and team
- Protect company information and assets
- Maintain work-life balance and well-being

### 4.2 Managers
- Approve remote work requests based on business needs
- Set clear expectations and performance goals
- Provide regular feedback and support
- Ensure team collaboration and communication
- Monitor productivity and address performance issues

### 4.3 IT Department
- Provide secure remote access tools and support
- Ensure endpoint security for remote devices
- Maintain VPN and remote connectivity
- Provide technical training and troubleshooting
- Monitor security compliance

### 4.4 HR Department
- Administer remote work agreements
- Provide guidance on remote work policies
- Support employee well-being and work-life balance
- Coordinate equipment and workspace setup
- Handle remote work-related employee relations

## 5. Procedures

### 5.1 Remote Work Request Process
1. Employee submits remote work request with business justification
2. Manager reviews job requirements and team dynamics
3. HR reviews policy compliance and eligibility
4. IT assesses technology and security requirements
5. Approval granted with documented agreement

### 5.2 Workspace Setup
1. Employee assesses home workspace suitability
2. Company provides equipment if required
3. IT configures secure remote access
4. Ergonomics assessment conducted
5. Workspace approved and documented

### 5.3 Security Setup for Remote Work
1. Multi-factor authentication configured
2. VPN client installed and tested
3. Security awareness training completed
4. Device encryption enabled
5. Regular security assessments scheduled

### 5.4 Performance Management
1. Performance goals established for remote work
2. Regular check-ins scheduled (weekly/monthly)
3. Progress tracking and documentation
4. Performance reviews conducted regularly
5. Adjustments made based on feedback

## 6. Compliance and Enforcement

### 6.1 Monitoring and Auditing
- Regular performance and productivity reviews
- Security compliance monitoring
- Technology usage and access logging
- Annual remote work program assessment
- Employee satisfaction surveys

### 6.2 Non-Compliance Consequences
- **Performance Issues**: Performance improvement plan
- **Policy Violations**: Verbal warning and retraining
- **Security Violations**: Temporary suspension of remote access
- **Repeated Violations**: Return to office requirement
- **Severe Violations**: Termination of remote work agreement

### 6.3 Exceptions
Exceptions to this policy require:
- Business justification documented
- Department head approval
- HR and IT review
- Additional controls or monitoring
- Regular reassessment of exception
